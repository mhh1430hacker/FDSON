# 5. Conclusion

This work provides a rigorous numerical and statistical characterization of a threshold-coupled fractionally dissipative oscillator network (FDSON). Utilizing a strict verification protocol enforced by extensive Monte Carlo ensembles and full tangent-space dynamics, standard thermodynamic narratives—such as self-organized criticality, first-order phase transitions, periodic Turing structures, and subcritical bistability—have been systematically ruled out.

Amidst these discarded classical paradigms, a highly robust, independent mathematical object remains: a structurally robust strange invariant set characterized by two positive Lyapunov exponents ($\lambda_1 = 0.120, \lambda_2 = 0.020$) and an internally consistent Kaplan-Yorke dimension ($D_{KY} \approx 2.035$). This chaotic attractor governs a continuous, correlation-dominated medium devoid of periodic structures, exhibiting an extended correlation length ($\xi \approx 34.20$) insulated from decay by the non-uniform spatial dissipation of the fractional exponent $\alpha = 1.5$. The FDSON model establishes a minimal, structurally robust framework for evaluating non-uniform dissipation and threshold constraints in dissipative oscillatory media.
