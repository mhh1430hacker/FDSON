## 4.1 Physics of the Correlation-Dominated Regimes
The numerical results extracted from the **FDSON** framework reveal a rich dynamical architecture. The collapse of the dominant wavenumber $k_0 \rightarrow 0$ and the mathematical divergence of the pattern wavelength $\Lambda \rightarrow \infty$ prove that the system actively resists classical spatial symmetry-breaking mechanisms, such as the standard Turing instability or modulational (Benjamin-Feir) phase instabilities. In traditional activator-inhibitor media, spatial pattern selection is driven by an asymmetry between the diffusion coefficients of the interacting fields; in the FDSON model, the interaction kernel is non-local, threshold-bound, and hybrid.

Rather than developing periodic spatial structures, the network organizes into a **correlation-dominated medium**. The defining property of this regime is its capacity to transmit continuous phase information and maintain long-range spatial coherence across a macroscopic correlation length ($\xi \approx 34.20$) that extends far beyond the direct coupling radius ($R_0=15.0$), without relying on rigid periodic structural constraints. Such behavior is highly unconventional for localized networks operating under uniform random density distributions.

## 4.2 Nonlinear Instability and Attractor Micro-Architecture
The stark contrast between the continuous linear stability analysis (which predicts the asymptotic stability of the homogeneous state due to $\operatorname{Re}\lambda(k) < 0$ across all wavenumbers) and the active, broadband energy spectrum observed in the empirical structure factor $S(k)$ provides a critical physical insight. This discrepancy demonstrates that the irregular dynamics in the FDSON framework are not driven by infinitesimal microscopic fluctuations, but are instead governed by a **fully nonlinear-driven instability**.

The fractional dissipation term $\gamma A^{1.5}$ acts as an amplitude-dependent structural sieve. In localized spatial patches where amplitudes are low, the dissipative damping decreases, allowing the threshold-bound coupling to amplify local phase energy. As the local amplitude balloons, the fractional dissipation terms grow nonlinearly, absorbing energy and arresting global synchronization. This localized dissipation gradient loops the system back into its chaotic invariant set, shielding the spatiotemporal complexity from collapsing into a trivial uniform state.

## 4.3 Classification of Crossovers vs Thermodynamic Transitions
The complete absence of uniform crossing signatures in the Binder cumulant $U_4$ plots across four distinct scales rules out attempts to map the model onto standard thermodynamic phase transition paradigms or claim traditional universality classes (such as Directed Percolation). This numerical evidence mandates that the parameter boundaries be classified as **finite-size crossover phenomena** regulated by localized density scales and boundary limits.

Concurrently, the strict unimodality of the asymptotic probability density function $P(R_\infty | \epsilon)$ isolates the model from subcritical bistability narratives. The network is bound to a **single global chaotic attractor**, possessing a structurally robust tangent space that channels highly disordered initial configurations toward an identical statistical destination, independent of the initial perturbation amplitude.

## 4.4 Model Comparison, Limitations, and Future Characterization
When evaluated against classic statistical physics frameworks, such as the Complex Ginzburg-Landau Equation (CGLE) or adaptive Kuramoto models, the FDSON model stands out as a distinct hybrid framework owing to its integration of non-integer dissipation with strict hard network thresholds and elastic coupling bounds.

However, certain methodological limitations must be acknowledged. While the converged positive exponents ($\lambda_1 = 0.120, \lambda_2 = 0.020$) demonstrate an active multidimensional stretching mechanism, classifying this invariant set permanently requires resolving three critical open questions in future investigations:
1.  **Direct Correlation Dimension ($D_2$) Calculations:** Implementing the *Grassberger–Procaccia* algorithm to monitor $D_2(N)$ up to $N=5000$. Saturation of $D_2$ will provide the definitive proof isolating a low-dimensional strange attractor independent of system size.
2.  **Singular Value Spectrum Decay (POD/SVD):** Quantifying the singular value decay $\sigma_n$ of the global covariance matrix. A sharp exponential decay conforming to $\sigma_n \sim e^{-n}$ will mathematically confirm the presence of an **Effective Inertial Manifold**, reducing the infinite-dimensional continuous field to a low-dimensional master ODE set.
3.  **Lattice Deformation Profiles:** Mapping the continuous coordinates onto rigid square or triangular lattices to verify parameter universality and test for hidden spatial symmetry-breaking channels under rigid boundary constraints.
